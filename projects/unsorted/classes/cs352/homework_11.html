  1. Please describe what type of evaluation design has been employed. Is it within or between subjects and why?

    The interfaces offered the two different groups were the same for all users. The testing variable was the different performance on the interface for a given test subject. The type of evaluation therefore is within subjects.

  2. Would a repeated measures ANOVA allow you to determine if the results support or refute the hypothesis?

    The analysis of variance is used when there are independent samples from more than two groups. In this particular test, there are four groups of data, but they are not independant.

  3. How might you use t-tests to analyze the above data in order to support or refute the hypothesis? Why is this application of the t-test appropriate?

    Assuming that the populations are normal and the variances are equal, a t-test gives a value that can test if a given null hypothesis is accepted or not. For this particular experiment the experimenter's belief is that the second interface is more effective than the first. In order to emply reject-support testing the null hypothesis, H0, is formulated to contradict the experiementer's belief. I.E. for μ1 as the mean time for completion using the first interface and μ2 as the mean time using the second interface, H0: μ1 ≥ μ2.

    The t-test would then attempt to disprove that hypothesis.

  4. Create an appropriate graphical presentation of the information in Table 1. Provide an explanation of what you are attempting to show with the particular graphical visualization and why your visualization supports your intentions.

    The graphical representation I would produce, had I time, is a bar and whisker plot showing the mean estimated by the t-test for the two interfaces and a 95% confidince interval for each. It would would allow the experimenter to visually verify if their hypothesis is rejected (thereby supporting their belief).
